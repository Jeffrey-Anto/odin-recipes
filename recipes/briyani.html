Briyani

Description

Biryani is a flavorful rice dish popular in South Asia and its diaspora, featuring rice, spices, and meat (chicken, beef, goat, lamb, fish, or shrimp) or vegetables. It's known for its aromatic spices like saffron, cardamom, and cumin, and often includes layers of rice, meat, and fried onions.

Biryani's origins trace back to Persia, and it has evolved into numerous regional variations with unique ingredients and cooking methods.
