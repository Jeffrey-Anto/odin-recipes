Gulab jamun

Description

Gulab jamun is a popular Indian dessert made from milk solids (khoya), flour, and then deep-fried and soaked in a sweet syrup flavored with cardamom and rose water according to culinary sites.

The name "gulab jamun" is derived from the Persian words "gulab" (rose) and "jamun" (a type of berry), referring to the rose-scented syrup and the berry-like shape of the dessert.

Ingredients

  • Gulab Jamun Mix
  • Milk (or water)
  • Oil or ghee
  • Sugar - 1½ cups
  • ginger-garlic paste
  • Cardamom – 2 (crushed)

Steps

  1. Prepare the sugar syrup
  2. Make the dough
  3. Make balls
  4. Fry the Jamuns
  5. Soak in sugar syrup
